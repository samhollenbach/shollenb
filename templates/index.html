Sam Hollenbach

Student / Software Developer

I am a Computer Science and Physics double major at Macalester College, where I also play varsity baseball (Graduation: May 2019). I have had a passion for programming since I was 13. Besides programming, I am a huge music fan and love going to live music. A Bay Area native, I’ve traveled all over the world and enjoy exploring new areas.

Video Games

I started programming in 2010 at age 13, making mods for the video game "Minecraft." Over the years I have made many video game add-ons and mods, and some small standalone games. Video games were the original reason for me picking up coding, and I have dreamed of running a game development studio. While my passion for video games persists, as I’ve diversified my skill set through coursework, projects and internships I’ve expanded my interests to many other domains of computer science.

Scientific Computing

As a programmer pursuing a degree in a quantitative science, I have always been drawn to the power of computation for scientific inquiry. My first true foray into this application of programming came in the form of an Independent Research project in 2016, in which I created a rudimentary simulation of a spiral galaxy. This sparked an appreciation for computer simulations and how they can merge theory and computation to provide insight into the physical world. Physical simulations also have a strong tie to video games, as nearly every game nowadays is built on some sort of physics engine. I’ve also applied my programming skills to bioinformatics, and recently completed a highly competitive NSF funded internship doing sunspot series reconstruction.

Web Dev

Lately, while completing coursework in internet computing, I have discovered an affinity for web development. I took the opportunity of an open final project to create a web interface to accompany a mobile app I have been working on. To create a well-functioning and scalable website I crafted my first REST API, which is also now being used by the mobile app. Although I enjoy front-end development work, back-end web development most excites me. However, I am working to improve my front-end skills as well, in part by making this website!
